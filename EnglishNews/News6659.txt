The Euro 2020 soccer final between England and Italy in July was a "superspreader" event due to the level of COVID-19 infection found in or around London's Wembley Stadium on the day, according to official data published on Friday.Public Health England said 2,295 people were likely to have been infectious with a further 3,404 people potentially acquiring infection at the July 11 match."Euros final was superspreader event," declared the Times newspaper.The match, with a crowd of around 67,000 inside the stadium, was England's first final in an international soccer tournament since the
